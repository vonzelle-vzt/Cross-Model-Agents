# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

**Project:** Cross-Model Adversarial Agents
**Maintainer:** VZT Tech Consulting
**Contact:** vonzelle@vzttechconsulting.com
**Last updated:** 2026-05-12

---

## 1. Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone — regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an **open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community** — including in technical discussions about AI tools, model behavior, and adversarial review patterns where opinions are often strong and reviews are often critical by design.

This project's whole purpose is adversarial review. Disagreement, pushback, and "you're wrong about X" are not just allowed — they're the product. But adversarial *ideas* and adversarial *people* are different things. This Code distinguishes them.

## 2. Our Standards

### 2.1 Behavior that contributes to a positive environment

- Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other people.
- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences — especially when reviewing someone else's code or design choices.
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive criticism. "This pattern is over-engineered because X" is fine. "You're a bad engineer" is not.
- Accepting responsibility and apologizing to those affected by our mistakes, and learning from the experience.
- Focusing on what is best not just for us as individuals, but for the overall community.
- Using welcoming and inclusive language. Default to clear, plain English; explain jargon when used.
- Citing sources, prior art, and reasoning when proposing technical changes.

### 2.2 Behavior that is unacceptable

- The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of any kind.
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal attacks.
- Public or private harassment, including stalking, sustained disruption, or unwelcome contact.
- Publishing others' private information — physical or email addresses, employer details, account handles, or any other personally identifying information — without their explicit permission.
- Sending unsolicited promotional material, recruiting messages, or commercial solicitations to community members.
- Deliberately introducing malicious code (including prompt injection payloads or model-jailbreak chains) into the codebase, agent prompts, or example files.
- Filing knowingly false bug reports, security advisories, or vulnerability claims.
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

### 2.3 Project-specific expectations

Because this is an AI-assisted development project, we also expect:

- **Honest attribution of AI-generated content.** If you used Claude, Codex, or another model to draft code or text in a contribution, that's fine — but do not misrepresent significant model output as wholly human-written when asked, and do not submit model output you have not actually read and understood.
- **Anti-slop discipline in your own contributions.** This project's reason for existing is to detect AI-generated bloat. Submissions that fail the project's own anti-slop gate are subject to revision requests — that's not a personal judgment, it's the system working as intended.
- **No model wars.** Constructive comparison of Claude vs. Codex vs. other models is welcome and useful. Tribalism ("Codex stans," "Claude fanboys," etc.) is not.
- **Respect for users with different subscription tiers.** Some contributors will have Anthropic API access but not OpenAI, and vice versa. Test plans and review feedback should not assume parity of access.
- **Security disclosures go to the maintainer first.** If you find a way to bypass the pipeline gates, leak prompts, or exfiltrate code via the agent layer, email vonzelle@vzttechconsulting.com — do not open a public issue.

## 3. Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders — currently the project maintainer at VZT Tech Consulting — are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior. They will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

## 4. Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces — including but not limited to:

- The GitHub repository (issues, pull requests, discussions, commit messages, code comments).
- Any community chat (Discord, Slack, etc.) operated under the project's name.
- Conference talks, blog posts, or social media posts where an individual is officially representing the project.
- Direct communication with the project maintainer about project matters.

It also applies when an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces. Examples of representing our community include using an official email address, posting via an official social media account, or acting as an appointed representative at an online or offline event.

## 5. Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, please report it to the project maintainer:

**Email:** vonzelle@vzttechconsulting.com
**Maintainer:** VZT Tech Consulting

When you report an incident, please include — to the extent you are comfortable:

1. **What happened** — a brief factual description.
2. **When and where** — date, time, and the platform (GitHub issue link, commit, channel, etc.).
3. **Who was involved** — usernames or names if known.
4. **Evidence** — links, screenshots, or copies of messages, if available.
5. **What outcome you are hoping for** — apology, removal of content, ban, mediation, etc. (We will not always be able to deliver the exact outcome you request, but knowing your hope helps us calibrate the response.)

All reports will be reviewed and investigated **promptly and fairly**. We aim to acknowledge receipt within 72 hours and provide an initial response within 7 days, though complex cases may take longer.

**Confidentiality:** The maintainer will respect the privacy and security of the reporter. Identifying details will not be shared with the accused or with the wider community without the reporter's explicit consent, except where required by law.

**No retaliation:** Retaliation against a reporter, witness, or anyone who participates in good faith in an investigation is itself a violation of this Code of Conduct and will be handled accordingly.

## 6. Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in determining the consequences for any action they deem in violation of this Code of Conduct.

### 6.1 Correction

**Community Impact:** Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed unprofessional or unwelcome.

**Consequence:** A private, written warning from a community leader, providing clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

### 6.2 Warning

**Community Impact:** A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

**Consequence:** A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, for a specified period of time. This includes avoiding interactions in community spaces as well as external channels like social media. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

### 6.3 Temporary Ban

**Community Impact:** A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence:** A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period of time. No public or private interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, is allowed during this period. Violating these terms may lead to a permanent ban.

### 6.4 Permanent Ban

**Community Impact:** Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment of an individual, or aggression toward or disparagement of classes of individuals.

**Consequence:** A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the community.

## 7. Conflicts of Interest

If a report concerns the project maintainer themselves, the reporter may escalate to a neutral third party at their discretion — for example, by emailing a separate address you trust, or by filing a public issue if the conduct in question is itself already public. We will work to establish a code-of-conduct committee as the project's contributor base grows.

If a maintainer has a personal or financial conflict of interest with a party to a report, they will recuse themselves from the investigation and seek a neutral arbiter.

## 8. Amendments

This Code of Conduct may be amended by the project maintainer. Significant changes will be announced via a `CHANGELOG.md` entry and a pinned issue or release note. Material changes that affect reporters' rights (e.g., changes to the reporting address, scope, or enforcement guidelines) will be communicated at least 14 days before taking effect, where reasonably possible.
